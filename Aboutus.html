About

Welcome to Rhythm House

Welcome to New Zealand event management and film production specialists, Rhythm House. With 20 years of experience – and literally hundreds of spectacular events and productions under our belts – we’re the company you can trust for your next event or film shoot. Whether you’re planning your upcoming public event, or a block buster movie, our team has the skills, networks, and creativity to take your vision to the next level. From coming up with creative concepts to putting together detailed event plans, Rhythm House has been at the forefront of showbiz in New Zealand and across the South Pacific. We’ve been the driving force behind some of the most successful Diwali festivals, the Holi Festival of Colours, Rangitoto College’s Style Me Fashion Show, numerous movie shoots and the cultural extravaganza that is Miss IndiaNZ – it’s been a hell of a ride! “Alongside commercial national and international event management and production services we’ve provided some amazing opportunities for local talent to launch careers in the entertainment industry. And we’ve got no plans of slowing down any time soon. So hit us up about your next project.”

Founder

Meet Mr. Dharmesh Parikh

At the helm of Rhythm House is Dharmesh Parikh, an acclaimed Events and Film producer. His proven skills and visionary approach make film production seem like a breeze. With an extensive network, professional acumen, and profound industry knowledge, Dharmesh ensures that Rhythm House remains the top choice for film production work in the South Pacific. Rhythm House founder and entertainment industry powerhouse Dharmesh Parikh got his first taste of showbiz as a young boy in a new country. “My family moved to New Zealand when I was 10, but to stay connected to our roots my brother and I watched endless Hindi films. How I longed to be part of that industry! I began taking Indian music classes, returning to India for a six-month stint to work with a top Indian music teacher. I got my first big break in 1996 performing with Bollywood’s Salman Khan and Dharmendra at Auckland’s Logan Campbell Centre. “That gave me a taste for showbiz. Then promoters started giving me coordinating responsibilities and I loved that even more. I began picking up more work as a coordinator than I did as a musician, helping out at local community events, Bollywood concerts and setting the foundation for the diversity stage for ASB Polyfest. In 2002 Rhythm House was born.”
